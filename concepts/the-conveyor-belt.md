# The Conveyor Belt

## 2026-03-02

The idea that since choosing to move from Italy to the US for college 12 years ago, there hasn't been a truly deliberate, from-within choice about the direction of life. Everything since has followed the default path for smart kids: good college → useful field → prestigious career.

- The last choice that really "came from within" was moving to the US in 2013 — and even that might have been more circumstantial than deliberate.
- MIT → D.E. Shaw was a peak outcome on the conveyor belt, especially given immigration constraints that made a startup path harder.
- The conveyor belt has produced good results. But is this what I actually want, or just where the belt took me?
- Open question: where does the belt go next, and do I want to stay on it?
