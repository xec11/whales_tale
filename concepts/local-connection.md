# Local Connection

## 2026-03-06

A longing for rootedness surfaced through two small moments over breakfast in Hawaii.

**The sour cherries.** Suzanne mentioned that in Germany they take sour cherries in early summer, let them sit in a jar of rum through fall and winter, and take them out in the spring. A simple thing, but the beauty of it spoke deeply — being in touch with the seasons, appreciating the world as it is and as it changes. A life measured in harvests rather than quarters.

**Japan by train.** A desire to explore Japan by train — small towns, temples, walking around, enjoying the interconnectedness. Not a vacation so much as a way of being in a place.

**Three models of human connection:**
- The lone wolf.
- The locally connected.
- The globally connected.

Big-city life is the third: depend on others for everything, but "others" means micro-interactions. You depend on any given person for a very small thing, yet depend on the system for everything. Maximally levered, and yet there is no human touch.

"Seeing the leverage makes me want to be more of a lone wolf, but in reality I think local connection is the answer."

**Connection to other concepts:**
- The conveyor belt delivered a globally connected life by default — MIT, New York, finance. Local connection would be a deliberate departure from that path.
- The safety spiral runs on rival goods (money, position). Local connection is built on non-rival ones (community, seasons, presence) — the kind of things "nobody will ever take away from me."
- Life-as-optimization: the optimizer gravitates toward global connection because it's scalable and measurable. Local connection resists measurement — its value is in the texture, not the output.
