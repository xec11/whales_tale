## After Breakfast

I have a couple of thoughts to write in here. Suzanne mentioned that in Germany they take sour cherries when they come out in the early summer and let them sit in a jar full of rum for the fall and winter, and take them out in the spring. It's a simple thing, but the beauty of it really spoke to me. I feel some longing for being in touch with the seasons, for appreciating the world the way it is and the way it changes.

This also made me think about another thing I would really like to do — explore Japan by train. Go to small towns, walk around, see the temples, enjoy the interconnectedness.

Another little hook. Human connection has three broad models:
* The lone wolf.
* The locally connected.
* The globally connected.

The latter is what we all are, in big cities. Depend on others for everything, but "others" is effectively micro-interactions where you only depend on any given person for a very small thing, while still depending on the system for everything. Maximally levered, and yet there is no human touch.

Seeing the leverage makes me want to be more of a lone wolf, but in reality I think local connection is the answer.
