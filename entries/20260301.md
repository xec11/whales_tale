# March 1, 2026 — Hawaii

## Morning

Just arrived in Hawaii on vacation today. A lot is going on.

As I left New York yesterday, the US and Israel struck Iran. I'm here feeling bad about the fact that I'll now have to think about that and "show commitment to work" during my vacation. I also feel a bit of hurt pride — this is kind of the worst-case scenario for strikes, and I had generally advocated that the risk premium in oil markets was too high. I had made it clear that I didn't dismiss the possibility of strikes killing the Supreme Leader of Iran, but I didn't think it was a good idea to protect against it. We have a lot of capacity to take risk, and I thought buying protection was negative alpha. My manager, Greg, was really worried and wanted to buy protection anyway, so we did. I feel a bit bitter about him being right, even though rationally I think it can well be true that sometimes low-probability events exist, protecting against them is expensive, but it can still turn out to be a good call after the fact.

I'm also speaking to a coach, Clive (The Sabbatical Coach), while I'm on vacation. I set that up because I've felt some desire — partially driven by value exploration done with Shirley, as I did some sample coaching sessions with her while she practices — to get clearer on what actually matters to me. I'm not sure where that will lead (take a sabbatical and come back? Quit completely? Take an extended break? Just keep going?), but the idea itself is bringing up a lot of uncertainty.

My job provides a lot of safety — it's very well paid and I don't think I could make nearly as much doing something else unless I took a lot of risk. The people are good. I often feel annoyed at Greg, who is pretty different from me in a lot of ways, but I think he's a good person, and most people around me are genuinely good people, both in general and for the finance industry in particular. I have the opportunity to work on really interesting stuff — thinking about markets and trying to find trading opportunities is exciting, and fits well with my desire to be a "safe investigator."

But there are things that are occasionally painful: stress, high responsibility, an advancement path that feels haphazard. Lately, what's been getting to me more is that because of all the geopolitical fires, I haven't had the energy and excitement to do the fun part of the work. There's also a bundling problem — the "investigator" and "debater" parts I really like; the "executor" and "firefighter" parts I hate. But I can't really have one without the other, since it's hard to have credibility in idea generation without also being an "actual trader" that puts on trades.

It particularly pains me to feel like I have to show commitment by doing something while on vacation because things are happening. It's like "virtue signaling" or "performative worrying." The truth is that what I'd actually want is to just be on vacation and take the results as they are when I'm back. I think that would be entirely possible and not damaging in real results terms. But I feel like it's just too hard not to feel the pressure. Maybe that's because Greg barely takes vacation, and when he does he's still obsessing over things. He doesn't demand that of people, but it's clear he generally believes in "the value of obsession" — which I fundamentally don't. Not in a rational sense (I know there are plenty of ways one can make more money if obsessed), more that I just never feel a personal compulsion to obsess over anything. It's more of a "sense of duty that has been put on me."

## Afternoon & Evening

The above was written in the morning, when I was stressed and upset — I hadn't yet spoken with my team, the market hadn't opened, and I was afraid it would all be a huge mess. I actually ended up having a very nice day.

Had a nice walk by the beach with Shirley, took our car back home just in time for me to hop on a team call. The market opened in a way that looked good both for me and for Greg — the position we were trying to protect didn't actually need protection (so I "was right"), and the protection we bought because he wanted it made money (so he "was also right"). Greg was in a good mood despite everything going on, which was a relief — I'd overly expected him to be upset. Obviously that was a narrow outcome, and I think it's still worth thinking about how important results are relative to expectations, but it was a relief nonetheless.

I also used the call to set boundaries pretty clearly: if I'm actually needed, I can log in, but I won't do work proactively while on vacation. This level feels right to me — proactive work is just performative and doesn't make me feel good. It's also not useful. On the other hand, this is a crisis, and if I can actually be helpful, I don't mind taking a little time off vacation to do targeted, productive things. I don't expect I'll actually be needed — we have a large-ish team now and I can only do so much from here anyway.

We also had a really pleasant day with our Hawaii family. After my call, I went to the beach and we had a nice swim. Then we did a little shopping, came home, and helped cook dinner. Delicious dinner, chatted for a while after, and went to bed.

## Reflection

Today was interesting in that my outlook really changed from morning to evening. On one hand, I'm happy it ended on a good note. On the other, I'm unhappy that my mood was so outcome-driven — I generally believe I should be more expectation-driven, and my expectations were off. But overall, a good day.
