# March 3, 2026 — Hawaii

## Greg's Offer

Another day in Hawaii. This morning I woke up to Greg's message (received around 4 AM) which acknowledged that I'd probably get it while asleep, but to let him know if and when I was available to talk after waking up. I messaged back when I woke up, at 6:40; when he called me at 7:30 or so he asked if I thought it'd make sense for me to work this week, and the firm would pay to move my flight a week out.

That's not actually that much of an issue for me — I'm here in a place we rented long-term, and Shirley is planning to stay a month or so, so I have flexibility. The pro: I work this week, probably can help while things are crazy, and can actually enjoy my vacation next week. The con: who knows if next week will be quiet, and even with my setup here I'm probably quite a bit less productive than I'd be at the office. Waking up at 4 AM would also not be awesome.

I think I handled it well. I thought about it, discussed with Shirley, and ended up offering three options:

1. I just keep my vacation. If I'm needed I can take a call, but I won't commit to going day by day without knowing if I'm expected to work or not, won't wake up at 4 AM, and won't take my laptop with me as I go around.
2. I work for the next three days from here, logging in at 4:30, and then base case take vacation next week, maintaining some flexibility in case needed. I can actually take a good shot at being productive this week, but I still need to take my vacation sooner or later.
3. I fly back today.

Greg and Natalia (the head of the team) ended up picking option 1, so I'm now free to enjoy my vacation — at least to the extent I can. That's probably the best outcome for me, although I'd have been fine with option 2 as well; option 3 would have been a pain.

I think overall this was handled fine. Of course it wasn't ideal — being on vacation during such a crazy week — but things happen and ultimately I'm not sure what could have been better besides just having picked a different week.

There's a bit of sadness and guilt about this, although on the other hand rationally I'm not beating myself up about it. It just is what it is.

## The Bundling Problem

What I don't like about it is mostly the feeling of pressure and anxiety. It made me think that maybe the startup idea — joining Michael and Suyash's startup once they get it off the ground — may just be a bad idea. Lots of firefighting at startups, lots of "you could always do more."

## The Coaching Text

Today, Shirley sent me this (from a random coaching WeChat group):

People are motivated to protect valued resources — safety, competence, belonging. In demanding roles, high achievers often overinvest in work to guard against anticipated loss.

When pressure repeatedly pairs with achievement, two systems overtrain:

- Amygdala–prefrontal threat activation (stress + control)
- Worry-based rumination loops (self-monitoring + overthinking)

Over time, pushing hard shifts from strategy to reflex. Even when pressure eases, the brain stays in "stay on" — draining energy, narrowing thinking, and straining relationships. Performance becomes costly.

"Just take a break" rarely interrupts this cycle.

The good news: this loop can be rewired.

Deliberate downshifting and recovery are essential. Resilience grows when activation is followed by recovery — not when activation stays constant.

Relational interventions matter — supportive leadership, attuned coaching, resource-rich HRM. Psychologically safe, meaningful moments at work shift stress circuits, restoring energy and adaptive capacity.

Resilience is not toughness; it is the capacity to regulate, recalibrate, and recover in ways that sustain performance.

Sustained excellence and wellbeing are cultivated, not squeezed.

It may sound AI-written (and it almost certainly is) but it definitely resonated with me to a good extent. If I take a step back, I'm doing too much or giving myself too hard a time relative to what I think is actually needed to maintain my situation where it is.

Too bad that the top part — the issue — resonates with me, and the bottom part — the loop can be rewired — feels like a bunch of nonsense speak. Like what does it even mean that resilience grows when activation is followed by recovery? That I should take a vacation and switch off after a crisis is done and over with?
