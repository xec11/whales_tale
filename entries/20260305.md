## After Lunch

The day got better; like the weather, my sadness slowly faded away. We went to Outrigger, I did a bit of vibecoding, and then we went to have sour soup at Hale Vietnam, then to get books at Da Shop and a matcha at Plantoem. I got a book on Japanese contentments. I had already seen the concept of ikigai, but thinking about it made me feel some sadness — albeit a "good kind" of sadness. The "it makes sense" kind of sadness. When I hear ikigai, these days I think about my AI game. But there's part of me that's afraid of making it a bigger part of my life — say if it meant that I devoted a lot of my sabbatical to it — how attached would I get to the outcomes? As it is right now, I enjoy it because it is, above all, something that I do out of desire for doing it, rather than for an outcome. I fear putting it into the box of things that are expected to do well.

The other thing that came up from that book is the idea that modern society, among various demands, expects one to be happy — not upset or sad or angry. I feel that — upon pain, there is suffering because there is part of me that feels like I should not be sad. But accepting sadness and anger is important too.
